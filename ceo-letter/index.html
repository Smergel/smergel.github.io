To Our Members, Stakeholders & Employees

At PJM, our top priority remains the reliability of the bulk power system within our footprint. This is a group effort that takes all of us working together to tackle complex problems that often don’t have clear or obvious solutions, but instead present a set of difficult tradeoffs. In such environments, broad stakeholder participation can be invaluable to help ensure our solution sets are pressure tested and reflect our collective best thinking. 2023 saw significant activity in our stakeholder process, with many of you coming together to help tackle the changes to PJM’s markets, planning and operations that are required as a result of the accelerating energy transition. Thank you for your effort, engagement and contribution!

2023 was a year of significant progress and momentum for PJM. In addition to ensuring bulk power system reliability through 2023, some highlights of the year include the following:

1. We placed significant focus on the emerging risk to resource adequacy during the energy transition, along with potential solutions. This effort included the publication of our resource adequacy analysis in the Energy Transition in PJM: Resource Retirements, Replacement & Risks (PDF) report as well as testimony in various state legislatures, the U.S. Senate and House, and other forums. PJM also partnered with other grid operators to provide comments to EPA on the potential implications of its proposed greenhouse gas rules on grid reliability. Further, PJM launched its multiyear Ensuring a Reliable Energy Transition initiative.

2. We initiated the transition to our new interconnection process, designed to accelerate the evaluation of new generation looking to interconnect to the grid, in July of 2023. In addition to approximately 40,000 MW that have already cleared the interconnection study process, in December of 2023, PJM determined that an additional 26,000 MW will be in the Expedited Process, and 46,000 MW are a part of the first transition cycle. These are expected to complete in 2024 and 2025 respectively.

I remain optimistic that, working together, PJM, our members and broader stakeholder community are well positioned to tackle the challenge of ensuring a reliable energy transition in our service footprint.

3. We completed a Critical Issue Fast Path process related to capacity market reforms and made two capacity market filings with FERC. One filing, which contained important market enhancements, including improved incorporation of winter risk in resource accreditation, a move to marginal Effective Load Carrying Capability for all resources and more frequent generator testing, was approved by FERC, while the second filing was not approved.

4. We spent a significant amount of time on activities related to Winter Storm Elliott, which occurred at the end of 2022. In 2023, PJM staff worked to respond to multiple rounds of FERC/NERC joint data inquiries and to perform an event analysis that included multiple recommendations moving forward. This event also required active management of credit risk and a filing with FERC to allow for the option for an extended time for the payment of Capacity Performance penalties related to the event. Our Finance Team developed processes for calculating and billing penalties, and our Legal, Markets and Operations teams helped achieve FERC approval of an uncontested settlement package that resolved virtually all complaints related to the storm.

5. 2023 was a year of significant transmission planning focus, with a record $6.8 billion of transmission projects approved by PJM’s Board. Further, PJM staff developed and actively sought member and stakeholder feedback on a framework for a potential scenario-based long-term regional transmission planning process.

While we collectively moved the ball down the field in several key areas in 2023, our work is not done. The energy transition appears to be accelerating, but there is growing uncertainty in the areas of policy, technology advancement on both the demand and supply sides of the market, consumer adoption of electric vehicles, the rate of growth of data center load, the impact of artificial intelligence, and more. These challenges are not unique to PJM – they are being felt by grid operators across the world. I remain optimistic that, working together, PJM, our members and broader stakeholder community are well positioned to tackle the challenge of ensuring a reliable energy transition in our service footprint.

Sincerely,

MANU ASTHANA
President & CEO
